Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Apostille? +

An Apostille is a certificate that authenticates the origin of a public document. It is issued by a competent authority in the state where the document was generated.

Do I need to be present for the Apostille process? +

No, you do not need to be physically present at the Secretary of State's office. You can simply mail us your documents or drop them off, and we handle the entire process.

What if the destination country is not in the Hague Convention? +

Documents going to non-member countries require a different, more complex legalization process through the specific country's embassy or consulate. We provide this service as well.
