Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to your questions about document legalization and notary procedures.

What is the difference between Notarization and Apostille? +

Notarization verifies the identity of the signer and the authenticity of their signature for domestic use. An Apostille is an international certification attached to the document by the Secretary of State, certifying it for use in countries that are members of the Hague Convention.

What documents can be authenticated for international use? +

Most public and private documents can be certified, including birth, marriage, and death certificates, degrees, diplomas, powers of attorney, corporate agreements, background checks, and passports.

Do I need to present original documents? +

Yes, for most apostille and legalization requests, the Secretary of State requires the original certified document issued by the local, state, or federal authority. For notarized copies, we must inspect the original document in person to certify the copy.
